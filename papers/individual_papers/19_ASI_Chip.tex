\begin{abstract}
We present the theoretical design for the Archetypal Synthetic Intelligence (ASI) Chip --- a quasicrystal holographic computing device whose material architecture is determined by the prime chronogram of the golden splitting primes $\{229, 181, 139, 59, 61, 71\}$. The chip operates by epiperiodic photonic transport: 11 incommensurate orbit frequencies, arising from the multiplicative orders of the golden ratio $\varphi$ and its conjugate $\bar\varphi$ across six prime fields, create a Cantor-set energy spectrum with critical wave functions identical to the Fibonacci tight-binding chain. We prove that aluminum (Z=13) serves as the number-theoretically neutral carrier (off the golden orbit, filling the $\mathbb{Z}/3\mathbb{Z}$ color quotient), that the composition Al$_{63}$Cu$_{25}$Fe$_{12}$ is stable because its multiplicative order in $\mathbb{F}_{229}^*$ equals the bitcollapse bound (57), and that the transport mechanism is mathematically indistinguishable from quantum coherent energy transfer in photosynthetic FMO complexes. We specify the fabrication environment (magnetron co-sputtering, 700--800$^\circ$C annealing, Ho magneto-optical readout, K photoelectric input) and propose falsifiable experiments.
\end{abstract}
